# Faction Style Bible — IronMarch Union

**Thesis:** the machine that keeps marching. Industrial command discipline — a faction that answers a dead world with foundries, rivets, and rank structure. If Neutral is the world as it died, IronMarch is the last institution refusing to admit it.

Deviations from the [game bible](00-style-bible.md) baseline:

## Accent

**Brass-gold.** Command braid, buttons, gun fittings, HQ trim, officer insignia, max-rank plating. Brass is a *command signal*: the more brass on a piece, the higher its authority tier (rifleman: buttons only → officer: braid + fittings → HQ: brass-crowned architecture). Never on large surfaces; a heavy tank earns a brass-lined turret ring, not a golden hull.

## Materials & palette

Gunmetal, riveted steel plate, olive wool greatcoats, oiled dark leather, coal-smoke black. Wear pattern: *maintained* — scratched and patched but oiled and regulation. IronMarch equipment is old, not neglected.

## Silhouette motifs

- **Vertical + angular.** Straight backs, squared pauldrons, flat-topped helmets, bayonet lines. The kitbash grammar: hard 90°/45° angles anywhere Dust Scourge would be ragged and Cartel would be draped.
- Heavy units get **visible mechanism** — pistons, exhaust stacks, tow chains. Dieselpunk means the machinery shows.
- Buildings: bunker mass + smokestack verticals; the HQ silhouette is a fortified foundry.

## VFX flavor

Standard-issue ordnance reads disciplined: tight muzzle flashes, straight tracers, drum-hit impacts. IronMarch-specific abilities (command, rally, artillery call-ins) flash **brass-gold**; the faction's saturation appears in its powers, not its paint.

## Do / Don't

Do: rivets, stenciled unit numbers, regulation kit repeated across squads (uniformity IS the identity). Don't: rags, trophies, asymmetric scavenge, occult markings — those belong to the other two factions; any drift toward them dilutes all three.
