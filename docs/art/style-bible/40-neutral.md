# Style Bible — Neutral

**Thesis:** the world as it died. Neutral is not a fourth faction — it is the game's visual baseline made walkable: pre-collapse military surplus, sandbags, field kit, and the militia still using them. Every faction bible is a deviation *from this document's look*.

**The hard rule (from CONTEXT.md):** Neutral pieces are usable by anyone — any player faction and the enemy pool, potentially both sides of the same battle. Therefore Neutral art must never intrinsically encode allegiance. No insignia, no faction accent, no side color anywhere in the sprite/model/texture. Side identity comes exclusively from the side channel (outline + base ring; arena spec §3).

## Accent

**None.** This is Neutral's identity, not a gap. The most saturated thing on a Neutral piece is bare steel glint or aged khaki. On a battlefield where every faction earns exactly one saturated accent, the pieces with *no* accent read instantly as unaligned — absence is the signal.

## Materials & palette

Khaki and bone canvas, worn olive drab, bare and dulled steel, weathered timber, sandbag burlap, dried-mud brown. Wear pattern: *abandoned then reused* — faded regulation kit, mismatched only by age, not by improvisation (that's Scourge's register).

## Silhouette motifs

- **Generic-soldier honesty.** Standard helmets, standard rifles, standard packs — deliberately the least distinctive humanoid silhouettes in the game, so faction silhouettes pop against them.
- Buildings and emplacements (supply depot, field hospital, MG nest, field gun): sandbag rings, timber frames, canvas tents — field-engineering shapes, not architecture. They read "position," faction HQs read "institution."
- Archetype silhouettes still must pass the black-shape test (game bible §2) — generic ≠ mushy.

## Enemy pools (palette entries, not bibles)

Enemy pools are dressed Neutrals/variants, not full factions:

- **Neutral Militia** — this document, fielded against you.
- **Crimson Legion** — heavy-assault variant: Neutral base + blood-red cloth accents and heavier plate. Red harmonizes with the enemy side channel; the Legion is always hostile, so the alignment is free reinforcement.
- **Ash Wraiths** — gas/ambush variant: pale ash-white wraps, bleached kit, low-value ghost read; pairs with the Ash Wraith fog-field arena theme.

If an enemy pool ever becomes playable, it graduates to a real faction bible and must be re-audited against the side channel (Crimson red especially).

## Do / Don't

Do: age, dust, faded stencils, sandbags, standard-issue everything. Don't: any faction accent color, any insignia, any silhouette flourish that would make a Neutral piece read as belonging to whoever fields it.
