# 0001 — Art identity: Darkest Dungeon skin on a Top Troops skeleton

Status: accepted (2026-07-10, greenfield art-plan session)

DeadManZone's visual identity blends two reference games that disagree on almost every axis. Rather than averaging them, each owns specific layers. **Top Troops owns structure:** bold archetype-coded silhouettes, stocky readable proportions, the combat camera/board staging, animation smoothness, and the bright saturated VFX/status color-coding layer. **Darkest Dungeon owns surface and mood:** desaturated war-worn base palette where saturation is reserved for what must win attention, inked outline treatment with varied stroke weight, grim environments, aged HUD texturing.

Why: mid-fight the game must read like a mobile autobattler (20+ units, statuses, instant parsing); everywhere else it must feel like a grim dieselpunk war. A DD-dominant blend was rejected because pooling-black ink fights small-sprite battlefield readability; a TT-dominant blend was rejected because a cheerful toy look fights the game's tone and the existing grimdark register.

Consequence: whenever legibility and mood conflict on the battlefield, legibility (the TT layer) wins; mood is recovered through palette, texture, and post — never by darkening the read.
