# 0005 — Per-unit combat morale with a rout-vs-kill economy; Manpower becomes run health

Status: accepted (2026-07-12 run-structure grilling; scheduled as its own milestone after the Dread run skeleton ships)

Every combatant carries a Morale bar beside HP; at zero it **Breaks** and routs — flees the field, removed from that fight without dying. A side is defeated when no unbroken, living units remain. Routed is not killed, and the economy hinges on that: enemy units routed grant **no salvage roll** (they escaped with their gear); player units routed survive the fight, cost no Manpower, and stand on their board slot again next round. Terror (morale-damage) builds are therefore tempo builds — faster, safer, poorer; kill builds are slower, bloodier, greedier. At run level, **Morale is deleted as a resource and Manpower becomes run health**: combat deaths deduct Manpower directly, defeat is being unable to field an army, Manpower-granting pieces are heals, and MoraleCalculator is retired. Rejected: army-level morale pool (a fifth of the cost, but mechanically just a second win-track — no per-unit rout stories, no rout-vs-kill economy), morale kept as a parallel run resource (two overlapping health bars at run scope), routed player units dying or needing redeployment (double-punishes the mercy mechanic).
